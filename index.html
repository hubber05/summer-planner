Affordable LSAT programs — ranked for 170+ from zero
★ Top pick
7Sage Core → Live
$69–$129/mo
~$447 summer total incl. LawHub · cancel anytime
50+ hrs of video lessons, 8,000+ question explanations, AI-powered analytics, daily live group classes (Live tier). Legendary among 170+ scorers. Start Core in June, upgrade to Live in July when you shift to timed practice. Self-directed but extremely rigorous.
7Sage Coach
$299/mo
~$1,017 for 3 months · personalized
Everything in Live plus a dedicated 99th-percentile coach, personalized study plan, and weekly accountability check-ins. Best if you want 1-on-1 guidance without Blueprint prices.
Magoosh LSAT
~$200 flat
100–150 hrs · fully self-paced
Cheapest serious option. Good video library. No live instruction. Best paired with LSAC official practice tests. Use as a supplement or if budget is very tight.
Blueprint Live Online
~$899–$1,300
100+ hrs · score improvement guarantee
Premium step up. Two-instructor live model, strong analytics. No 170 guarantee at this tier. If budget loosens mid-summer after a coaching paycheck, this is the natural upgrade from 7Sage.
Blueprint 170+
~$1,599–$2,249
70+ hrs live · 170+ score guarantee
The guarantee is the product. If you miss 170, retake the course free or get a refund. Best if you want maximum structure and insurance. Expensive but the retake guarantee has real value if you need multiple prep cycles.
Cost vs. retake math
7Sage Core → Live (summer) + 1 test~$695 total
7Sage + 2 test attempts~$943 total
Blueprint 170+ + 1 test~$1,847 total
Blueprint 170+ + 2 tests (no extra tuition)~$2,095 total
Each LSAT registration = $248. 7Sage is almost always cheaper even with multiple attempts.
Bottom line: 7Sage is cheaper in nearly every scenario. The only case Blueprint wins on value is if you need 6+ months of live classes AND miss 170 on multiple attempts and use the guarantee. For a disciplined self-studier with a structured summer schedule, 7Sage is the right call.

Law schoolStrongest profile
Apply as early in the cycle as possible
T14 schools use rolling admissions. An Oct 1 application competes against fewer applicants than a Dec 1 one — same LSAT, same GPA, meaningfully better odds.
June LSAT is the ideal first-attempt date
Scores release by July 1. That gives you August or September for a retake — still early enough to submit applications in October. Never make November your first attempt.
Your work narrative is your edge
Paralegal work, legal journalism, and paralegal coffee chats all feed directly into your personal statement. Document everything — what cases you observed, what stories you covered, what specifically drew you toward law.
LSAT score is the single biggest lever
At T14 schools, a 174 vs. a 170 can mean hundreds of thousands of dollars in scholarship difference. Don't rush. Take as long as you need to hit your target score.
Spanish + Arabic app are a distinctive narrative
Law schools love applicants with genuine skills and global perspective. Conversational Spanish + a tech app you built = compelling, unusual extracurricular story, especially for international law, immigration, or human rights tracks.
You can take the LSAT up to 5 times in a 5-year window. Most schools report only your highest score in their 509 data — retaking is low-risk if you have a clear prep plan.
